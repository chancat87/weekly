## Front-End Technology

**What the Astro Release Means for the Industry**  
<https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/G7b9Dv5xEvnM0AMT1EXKJQ>  
I’ve been tracking Astro for a while now; its "Islands architecture" is a brilliant way to ship less JS. they just raised $7M, which is a huge vote of confidence in the future of static-first web development.

**Optimizing Screen Sharing in RTC Scenarios**  
<https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/tyD_y0xhCMEJg0tfTelHfA>  
A technical breakdown from the Feishu (Lark) team on how they improved the latency and clarity of screen sharing in real-time communication.

**Deep Dive into pnpm**  
<https://segmentfault.com/a/1190000042318738>  
pnpm stands for "Performant NPM," and it lives up to the name. by using content-addressable storage and hard links, it saves disk space and eliminates "phantom dependencies." a must-try for any modern project.

**The Curious History of the HTTP Referer**  
<https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/HfOstfIdWsjNargulh5q5g>  
A look at the security and tracking implications of the Referer header—and yes, the name genuinely comes from a misspelling of "referrer" back in the early days of the web.

## Trending Open Source

**vim-easycomplete: The Best Autocompletion for VIM**  
<https://github.com/jayli/vim-easycomplete>  
If you’re a Vim user, this plugin is a game-changer. it’s fast, intuitive, and works like a charm.  
<img src="https://gw.alipayobjects.com/zos/k/u9/1.gif" style="width:600px"/>

**FluentUI Emoji: Microsoft’s Modern Open-Source Set**  
<https://github.com/microsoft/fluentui-emoji>  
Microsoft has open-sourced their beautiful, 3D-styled emoji library. they’re incredibly high-quality and free to use in your own projects.  
<img src="https://gw.alipayobjects.com/zos/k/1t/ozI6rx.jpg" width="800" />

**uchinoko: Generate a Cartoon Version of your Cat**  
<https://uchinoko-maker.jp/>  
A fun little tool for pet owners. I tried it out and made a perfect little cartoon of my cat, Kele.  
<img src="https://gw.alipayobjects.com/zos/k/sn/LVJbJR.jpg" width="800" />

**Atomico: Web Components with JSX**  
<https://github.com/atomicojs/atomico/>  
A developer-friendly way to build standard Web Components using a React-like JSX syntax. the project site is also very well-designed.
