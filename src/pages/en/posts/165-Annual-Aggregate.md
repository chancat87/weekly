## Learning Resources

1. [**Language Reactor**](https://www.languagereactor.com/): A fantastic tool for language learning through movies and TV shows.
2. [**YouGlish**](https://youglish.com/): Improve your English pronunciation using YouTube, showing how people speak in real-world contexts.
3. [**Periodic Table PRO**](https://pt.ziziyi.com/): A beautiful, visual periodic table for science enthusiasts.
4. [**Dribbble**](https://dribbble.com/): My go-to for design inspiration and aesthetic improvement.
5. [**Meiye**](https://www.meiye.art/): A design recommendation platform with excellent localized content.
6. [**Supercreative**](https://supercreative.design/): A collection of 1000+ inspiring websites for when you're stuck.
7. [**MuscleWiki**](https://musclewiki.com/): A visual guide to muscle-building exercises for specific body parts.
8. [**Every Programmer Should Know**](https://github.com/mtdvio/every-programmer-should-know): A massive collection of technical knowledge every software developer should have—CS, algorithms, distributed systems, etc.

## Audio & Visual

1. [**SkylineWebcams**](https://www.skylinewebcams.com/): Global HD live feeds—from the Western Wall to volcanoes in Etna or elephants in South Africa.
2. [**EarthCam**](https://www.earthcam.com/): Live views of cityscapes, landmarks, and even zoo/aquarium cameras around the world.
3. [**City Walks**](https://www.citywalks.live/): Virtual walks in cities worldwide—great for getting a feel for local vibes.
4. [**NASA Eyes**](https://eyes.nasa.gov/): A 3D space explorer to track planets, satellites, and spacecraft in real-time.
5. [**Radio Garden**](http://radio.garden/): Explore world radio stations via a 3D globe.
6. [**Music For Programming**](https://musicforprogramming.net/): Curated mixes designed to help you focus and find your flow state while coding.

## Tools

1. [**Shots**](https://shots.so/): Create beautiful, professional-looking screenshots with custom backgrounds.
2. [**Upscayl**](https://www.upscayl.org/): A handy AI-powered image upscaler.
3. [**AllinOneTools**](https://allinone.tools/): A one-stop shop for converting between any file format (PDF, images, video, etc.).
4. [**IT Tools**](https://it-tools.tech/): An online "Swiss Army Knife" for developers with tons of handy utilities.
5. [**WakaTime**](https://wakatime.com/): Track your coding time across various IDEs.
6. [**OrbStack**](https://orbstack.dev/): A fast, light, and beautiful alternative to Docker and Linux for Mac.
7. [**Bio Link**](https://bio.link/): A simple "link in bio" page to showcase your work.
8. [**Reactive Resume**](https://rxresu.me/): A free, open-source resume builder.
9. [**Nomad List**](https://nomadlist.com/): Data on thousands of cities for digital nomads—cost, Wi-Fi speed, and quality of life.

## Materials & Assets

1. [**macOSicons**](https://macosicons.com/#/): 5000+ free icons for macOS and iOS to keep your app icons consistent.
2. [**Pexels**](https://www.pexels.com/zh-cn/): Free, high-quality stock photos and videos.
3. [**Loose Drawing**](https://loosedrawing.com/): High-quality, commercially free illustrations by a Japanese designer.
4. [**ManyPixels**](https://www.manypixels.co/): Refined vector illustrations with dark mode support.
5. [**Mockups Design**](https://mockups-design.com/): Free, high-quality PSD mockups for 3D objects, brochures, and cards.
